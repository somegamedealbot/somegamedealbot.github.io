Prediction

Calculations

  • Primary Equation : \(V = \pi r^2 h\)
    (Volume of a cylinder)
  • Secondary Equation : \(S = 2 \pi r^2 + 2 \pi h\)
    (Surface area of a cylinder)
Surface Area: $$S = {2 \pi (5.08)^2 + 2 \pi (5.08) (14.732)}$$ $$S = {2 \pi (3.81)^2 + 2 \pi (3.81) (10.16)}$$
$$S \approx {632.4 cm^2}$$ $$S \approx {334.4 cm^2}$$
Volume: $$V = {\pi (5.08)^2 (14.732)}$$ $$V = {\pi (3.81)^2 (10.16)}$$
$$V \approx 1194.4 cm^3$$ $$V \approx 463.3 cm^3$$
Solving for h: $$632.4 = {2 \pi r^2 + 2 \pi r h}$$ $$334.4 = {2 \pi r^2 + 2 \pi r h}$$
$$632.4 - 2 \pi r^2 = {2 \pi r h}$$ $$334.4 - 2 \pi r^2 = {2 \pi r h}$$
$${632.4 - 2 \pi r^2 \over 2 \pi r} = {h}$$ $${334.4 - 2 \pi r^2 \over 2 \pi r} = {h}$$
Plugging h back to Volume: $$V = {\pi r^2 ({632.4 - 2 \pi r^2 \over 2 \pi r})}$$ $$V = {\pi r^2 ({334.4 - 2 \pi r^2 \over 2 \pi r})}$$
$$V = {316.2 r - \pi r^3}$$ $$V = {167.2 r - \pi r^3}$$
$$V' = {316.2 - 3 \pi r^2}$$ $$V' = {167.2 - 3 \pi r^2}$$
Setting V' = 0:
(Finding when V is at max)
 $$316.2 = {3 \pi r^2}$$ $$167.2 = {3 \pi r^2}$$
$${316.2 \over 3 \pi} = {r^2}$$ $${167.2 \over 3 \pi} = {r^2}$$
$$\sqrt {316.2 \over 3 \pi} = {r}$$ $$\sqrt {167.2 \over 3 \pi} = {r}$$
$$5.8 cm \approx r$$ $$4.2 cm \approx r$$
Solving for h: $$632.4 = {2 \pi (5.8)^2 + 2 \pi (5.8) h}$$ $$334.4 = {2 \pi (4.2)^2 + 2 \pi (4.2) h}$$
$$632.4 = {67.28 \pi + 11.6 \pi h}$$ $$334.4 = {35.28 \pi + 8.4 \pi h}$$
$$632.4 - 67.28 \pi = {11.6 \pi h}$$ $$334.4 - 35.28\pi = {8.4 \pi h}$$
$${632.4 - 67.28 \pi \over 11.6 \pi}= { h}$$ $${334.4 - 35.28 \pi \over 8.4 \pi}= { h}$$
$$11.6 cm \approx h$$ $$8.5 cm \approx h$$
Finding Maximum Volume for Each Can: $$V = {\pi (5.8)^2 (11.6)}$$ $$V = {\pi (4.2)^2 (8.5)}$$
$$V \approx 1225.9 cm^3$$ $$V \approx 471.1 cm^3$$
Percentage of being 'volume-optimized: $${1194.4 cm^3 \over 1225.9 cm^3} * 100$$ $${463.33 cm^3 \over 471.1 cm^3} * 100$$
$$\approx 97.4 \% $$ $$\approx 98.4 \% $$

Analysis

1. The percentages calculated in part D reflects the design of the cans, shows if they effectively used material to get the most volume within a can.

2. A can being 'volume-optimized' means that its dimensions allows the can's volume to be as big as it can be based on a given amount of materials.

3. My prediction of the chicken soup base can winning was incorrect as it was 97.4% volume-optimized and was beaten by the chicken broth being 98.4% volume-optimized. These results didn't really surpise me since I knew it would be close since companies would want to get the most out of the materials used to cut costs and gain profit.
